## Why Each Attribute Was Chosen

### Horsepower (hp)

    This Attribute favours the raw engine power and is one of the most favoured performance stat. Ranking it the highest will result in exciting and competitive which also reflects how people compare cars in real life.

### Top Speed (km/h)

    Top Speed also favours performace which rewards well engineered vehicles. It complements horsepower without being identical to it. 

### Acceleration (0–100 km/h)

    The Acceleration attribute will change things up with the fact that a car can have high horsepower but poor acceleration which prevents any single car from dominating every round. 

### Safety Rating (ANCAP Stars)

    This attribute introduces a non-performance dimension where practical family cars can score well here. This creates a balancing factor between sports cars and generic cars.


### Fuel Efficiency (L/100km)

    This attribute encourages environmental thinking. Lower values win, which means economy cars can compete with less environmental friendly cars creating additional balancing factors to the game. 

### Market Value (AUD $)

    This one is ranked last because MArket Value shouldn't be the sole deciding factor for a winner since an expensive car doesn't necessarily make it better. This also prevents luxury brands form automatically dominating.
